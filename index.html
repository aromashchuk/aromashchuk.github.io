FAQ

Questions, answered.

Do I have to sign a long-term contract?

No. We start with a 20-hour minimum engagement so I can deliver something real. After that you can scale up, move to a monthly retainer, or pause — whatever fits.

How is this different from offshore QA agencies?

I'm US-based and work in your timezone, so communication is same-day and direct. You talk to the person actually testing your product — no account managers, no rotating juniors, no overnight lag.

Manual testing or automation — which do I need?

Usually both, in the right order. We'll figure that out in the free audit. Automation pays off for repetitive regression flows; hands-on QA catches the messy edge cases. I won't sell you a framework you don't need yet.

What does the free QA audit include?

A 30-minute call where I look at your product and current process, identify your biggest quality risks, and recommend the smallest next step that moves the needle. No obligation.

How many clients do you take on at once?

Only a few at a time. I cap active projects so each one gets my direct, senior attention — and I'm growing a small US-based team to extend that without diluting it. If I'm at capacity, I'll tell you honestly and we'll line up a start date that works.

Do we work by contract or hourly?

Either. Most teams run a monthly retainer or a fixed-scope contract for predictable cost; straight hourly is available when that fits better. We'll pick whatever suits your stage.

What's your pricing?

Hourly rates start at $50–$95 depending on the work. Most clients move to a predictable monthly retainer or a fixed-scope quote after the first engagement. I'll give you a clear number before we start.
